## Overview

Datong's Ancient City (大同古城) is a 3.28 sq km walled city — one of the best-preserved Ming Dynasty urban layouts in northern China. The walls you see today were rebuilt in the early 2000s, but they follow the exact footprint of the original 1372 fortifications built by General Xu Da.

Inside the walls: temples, mosques, churches, pagodas, courtyards, noodle shops, and a living city where people still wake up, buy vegetables, and walk their kids to school. It's not a museum — it's Datong's real downtown.

<InfoBox type="highlight">
Don't just walk the main streets. The best parts of the Ancient City are the alleys between the sights — courtyard homes with red lanterns, old men playing chess, the smell of knife-cut noodles drifting from a tiny shop with three tables.
</InfoBox>

## Recommended Walking Route (Clockwise Loop)

### Morning
1. **Four Archways (四牌楼)** — The exact center. Four ornate Ming Dynasty gateways mark the crossroads. Start here to orient yourself.
2. **Guandi Temple (关帝庙)** — Yuan Dynasty hall dedicated to Guan Yu, the God of War. Pre-Ming timber joinery, red-faced statue, green dragon blade.
3. **Chunyang Palace (纯阳宫)** — Daoist temple with an 800-year-old cypress tree and a courtyard designed to make you linger.
4. **The Great Mosque (清真大寺)** — Founded in the Yuan Dynasty. Chinese roof + Arabic calligraphy. 700 years of Muslim life inside Han-majority walls.

### Lunch Break
5. **The Catholic Church (天主堂)** — Belgian missionaries, 1889. Gothic Revival in grey Chinese brick. One of the strangest and most beautiful buildings in Shanxi.
6. **Wen Miao (文庙)** — The Confucian Temple. No incense, no statues — just scholars' names carved in stone tablets. Imperial yellow roof tiles (only Confucius got that privilege).

### Afternoon
7. **Shanhua Temple (善化寺)** — Jin Dynasty, 1,200 years old, and still uncrowded. Portrait-like Buddha faces and Liao Dynasty murals most visitors never notice. ¥50 entry.
8. **Fahua Temple (法华寺)** — White marble Yuan Dynasty pagoda hidden in a quiet courtyard.
9. **Dongnan Yi (东南邑)** — The trendy quarter. Boutiques, craft shops, cocktail bars, Western restaurants. Young Datong's hangout.
10. **Nine Dragon Screen (九龙壁)** — 45m of glazed ceramic dragons. 1392. Cobalt blue from Persia. All that remains of a Ming prince's palace.

### Sunset
11. **The City Wall** — Start at the South Gate. Walk the 1.5km stretch to the Drum Tower. 14 meters up, the best view in Datong: Huayan's pagoda to the west, Yungang 16km beyond, the sun setting over the mountains.

<InfoBox type="tip" title="Pro Tip">
Rent a bike on the city wall (¥30/hour). The full loop is 7km — walking it all takes 2+ hours, but cycling is 30 minutes of pure joy, especially at sunset.
</InfoBox>

## Why It Matters

Most Chinese cities tore down their walls in the 20th century. Datong rebuilt theirs — and inside, a living city still breathes. Temples, mosques, churches, and noodle shops sharing the same 3.28 sq km. It's not a theme park version of old China. It's the real thing, still figuring itself out.
