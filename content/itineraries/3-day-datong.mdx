## Overview

This 3-day itinerary covers Datong's essential sights at a comfortable pace. You'll see two UNESCO-level attractions, walk a Ming Dynasty city wall, and eat more noodles than you thought possible.

**Who this is for:** Independent travelers who want to see the highlights without rushing.
**Best for:** A long weekend from Beijing (2h by train) or as a stop on a longer Shanxi trip.

<InfoBox type="highlight">
**Quick Summary:**
- Day 1: Yungang Grottoes + Huayan Temple + City Wall sunset
- Day 2: Hanging Temple + Hunyuan village + Shanhua Temple
- Day 3: Yingxian Wooden Pagoda + Nine Dragon Screen + food exploration
</InfoBox>

## Day 1: Grottoes & Ancient City

### Morning: Yungang Grottoes (8:30 AM – 12:30 PM)

Arrive at the grottoes at opening time (8:30 AM). You'll beat the tour buses and have the best caves almost to yourself for the first hour.

**Route through the caves:**
1. Start at Caves 1–4 (20 min) — warm up with the smaller caves
2. Caves 5–6 (30 min) — the 17m Buddha and the life-of-Buddha carvings. The highlight.
3. Caves 7–12 (30 min) — the Music Cave (Cave 12) is unmissable
4. Caves 16–20 (30 min) — the Tan Yao Caves, including the iconic open-air Buddha at Cave 20
5. Yungang Museum (30 min) — air conditioning + excellent English exhibits

**Budget:** ¥120 (entry) + ¥20 (audio guide, worth it) + ¥15 (golf cart from entrance, optional)

### Lunch: Dongfang Xiaomian (东方削面)

Take Bus 3 or a taxi back toward the city. Stop at **Dongfang Xiaomian (东方削面)** — the most famous knife-cut noodle shop in Datong. Order:

- A bowl of **刀削面** (dāo xiāo miàn) with braised pork — ¥15–20
- A side of **卤蛋** (lǔ dàn, braised egg) — ¥3
- A bottle of local **沙棘汁** (shā jí zhī, sea buckthorn juice) — ¥5

The noodles are shaved directly from a dough block into boiling water in front of you. Chewy, irregular, perfect.

### Afternoon: Huayan Temple (2:00 PM – 3:30 PM)

Back in the ancient city, head to **Huayan Temple (华严寺)**. This is the largest and best-preserved Liao Dynasty (907–1125) temple complex in China.

**Don't miss:**
- The **Main Hall** — one of the largest Buddhist halls in China, with Ming Dynasty frescoes covering every wall
- The **Bhagavan Sutra Hall** — exquisite Liao Dynasty wooden cabinets for storing sutras, with intricate joinery
- The **Five Buddha statues** in the upper hall — surprisingly expressive faces

**Budget:** ¥60

### Late Afternoon: Explore the Ancient City (3:30 PM – 5:30 PM)

Wander the restored Ming/Qing streets around the Drum Tower (鼓楼). This area was heavily renovated in the 2010s — it's less "authentic" than Pingyao but still pleasant, with tea houses, craft shops, and street food.

Pop into **Shanhua Temple (善化寺)** if you have energy — it's free and less visited than Huayan, with a serene garden courtyard.

### Evening: City Wall at Sunset + Dinner

Rent a bike at the city wall (¥30) and ride the **7.2km loop** as the sun sets. The wall is wide enough for a car lane and offers panoramic views of the old and new city. Best photo spot: the south gate (永泰门) looking north toward the Drum Tower.

**Dinner options:**
- **Fenglinge (凤临阁)** — Upscale Shanxi cuisine in a beautifully restored courtyard. Try the **百花烧麦** (flower-shaped shaomai). ¥80–120/person.
- **Street food near the Drum Tower** — Grilled skewers (串儿), stuffed pancakes (馅饼), and candied hawthorn (糖葫芦). ¥20–40.

**Overnight:** Stay inside or near the ancient city walls.

## Day 2: The Hanging Temple

### Morning: Drive to Hanging Temple (7:30 AM – 9:00 AM)

Leave Datong at 7:30 AM. Arrange a private car through your hotel (¥400–600 round trip) or take the bus from Datong Bus Station (¥30, 2 hours, then minibus ¥20 to temple).

Arrive by 9:00 AM — this gives you the best light for photos and fewer crowds.

### Late Morning: Hanging Temple Climb (9:00 AM – 11:00 AM)

The temple opens at 8:00 AM. Buy the full ticket (¥130) if you're climbing, or the ground-view ticket (¥15) if you prefer to stay below.

**The climb takes 30–45 minutes.** It's narrow, steep in places, and the railings are low. The views from the top walkways are spectacular — you're 75m above the canyon floor, looking down at the river and up at the cliff face.

<InfoBox type="warning">
Skip the climb if you have vertigo, mobility issues, or children under ~8. The best photos are from the ground anyway.
</InfoBox>

### Lunch: Hunyuan Village (11:30 AM – 12:30 PM)

Drive 10 minutes to Hunyuan town. Find a small restaurant and order:

- **浑源凉粉 (Húnyuán liángfěn)** — Cold bean jelly noodles with sesame paste, vinegar, and chili. Hunyuan's signature dish. ¥10–15.
- **刀削面** — Because you can't have too many noodles. ¥15.
- **Local tofu** — Shanxi tofu is firmer and nuttier than southern styles. ¥15–20.

### Afternoon Option A: Mount Heng (1:00 PM – 4:00 PM)

If you're feeling energetic, Mount Heng (恒山) is 5km from the Hanging Temple. Take the cable car (¥70 one way) to the mid-mountain temples, then hike 40 minutes to the summit (2,017m). The Daoist temples along the way are modest compared to the Hanging Temple, but the mountain views are worth it.

### Afternoon Option B: Return to Datong (1:00 PM – 2:30 PM)

If you skip Mount Heng, drive back to Datong and use the extra time to explore:

- **Datong Museum (大同博物馆)** — Free, modern, excellent English signage. Covers Datong's history from the Northern Wei capital through the coal mining era. 1–1.5 hours.
- **Coal Mine Museum (晋华宫矿山公园)** — A retired coal mine turned museum. You can go underground (hard hat provided). Quirky and fascinating. ¥60. 1.5 hours.

### Evening: Datong Night Market

Near the Drum Tower, the night market comes alive after 6 PM. Try:

- **羊肉串 (yáng ròu chuàn)** — Lamb skewers with cumin and chili, grilled to order. ¥3–5 per skewer.
- **烤冷面 (kǎo lěng miàn)** — Griddled cold noodles with egg, sausage, and sweet sauce. A northeastern Chinese street food staple. ¥8.
- **Local beer** — Yanjing (燕京) or Tsingtao (青岛), ¥8–15 per bottle.

**Overnight:** Same as Day 1.

## Day 3: Wooden Pagoda & Hidden Gems

### Morning: Yingxian Wooden Pagoda (8:00 AM – 12:00 PM)

Take a 1-hour drive south to Yingxian (应县) to see the **Sakyamuni Pagoda (应县木塔)**, the oldest and tallest wooden pagoda in the world.

Built in **1056 AD** during the Liao Dynasty, it stands 67 meters tall and was constructed **without a single nail** — entirely with interlocking wooden brackets (dougong, 斗拱). It has survived 40+ major earthquakes and still stands, slightly tilted (about 2° off vertical).

**Budget:** ¥60 entry. You can only go up to the second floor now (structural preservation).

**Getting there:** Private car from Datong (¥300–400 round trip) or bus from Datong Bus Station (¥25, 1.5 hours).

<InfoBox type="tip" title="Local Tip">
After seeing the pagoda, walk through Yingxian's old town — it's a sleepy, non-touristy Shanxi county that gives you a glimpse of real life outside the cities. The morning market near the pagoda sells fresh walnuts, dates, and Shanxi aged vinegar (山西老陈醋) — great edible souvenirs.
</InfoBox>

### Lunch: Back in Datong (1:00 PM)

Return to Datong for a final feast:

- **Datong Hot Pot (大同火锅)** — Northern Chinese style, with lamb and pickled cabbage. Try **老北京涮羊肉** or any local hot pot spot with a queue.
- Or keep it casual with more knife-cut noodles — you'll miss them when you leave.

### Afternoon: Last Sights (2:30 PM – 5:00 PM)

Fit in anything you missed:

- **Nine Dragon Screen (九龙壁)** — The oldest and largest glazed-tile dragon screen in China (built 1392). ¥10. 30 minutes. Worth it for the ceramic craftsmanship.
- **Datong City Wall daytime walk** — Different vibe from the sunset ride. Less crowded, good for photography.
- **Last-minute food shopping** — Buy Shanxi aged vinegar (山西老陈醋) and dried noodles as gifts. Available at any supermarket.

## If You Have More Time

- **4 Days:** Add a morning at the Coal Museum + afternoon tea in a traditional courtyard
- **5 Days:** Day trip to **Wutaishan (五台山)**, one of China's Four Sacred Buddhist Mountains (2.5 hours from Datong)
- **1 Week:** Datong → Pingyao (2.5h train) → Xi'an (3.5h train). The classic Shanxi-Shaanxi route.

## If You Only Have 1 Day

Do Yungang Grottoes in the morning (8:30 AM–12:30 PM), lunch at Dongfang Xiaomian, then Huayan Temple + Nine Dragon Screen + City Wall in the afternoon. Miss the Hanging Temple — it's too far for a day trip unless you have a car and skip everything else.
