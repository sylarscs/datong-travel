# The City Wall at Sunset

**Duration:** ~3 minutes
**Location:** City wall, south gate (永泰门)
**Order:** Episode 10 of 10 (closing)

---

## Script

You've walked 5 kilometers, visited a dozen temples, a mosque, and a church. You've crossed the spiritual geography of an entire city in an afternoon. Now, climb the wall.

The ramp at Yongtai Gate brings you up to the top of the fortifications — 14 meters above street level, looking out over the ancient city on one side and modern Datong on the other. The contrast is stark: grey-tiled roofs and narrow lanes inside the wall; glass towers and eight-lane roads outside. Datong is two cities sharing one name, and this wall is the seam.

The wall itself runs 7.2 kilometers. You don't need to walk all of it. The section from Yongtai Gate east to the Drum Tower gate is the best — about 1.5 kilometers, with the best views and the fewest tour groups. Rent a bike (¥30) or walk. At sunset, the brickwork turns gold, the city lights begin to flicker on, and the wall — built for war — becomes something else entirely: a promenade, a panorama, a place to sit and let the day settle.

From up here, you can trace the route you walked: the Drum Tower directly ahead, the Bell Tower behind it, the Four Archways at the intersection. Huayan Temple's pagoda rises to the west. And beyond the walls, 16 kilometers away, the cliff where Yungang's 51,000 Buddhas face the setting sun.

Datong was a military city before it was anything else. For centuries, soldiers stood where you're standing, watching the northern horizon. The view has changed — the Mongol armies aren't coming — but the wall remains. It's still here. Like everything else in this city, it has outlasted its original purpose and found a new one.

Thanks for walking with us. If you haven't yet, the Yungang Grottoes, Hanging Temple, and Huayan Temple audio guides are available on this site. Datong rewards the curious. You've made a good start.

## Quick Facts

- **Height:** 14 meters above street level
- **Best section:** Yongtai Gate to Drum Tower gate (~1.5 km)
- **Bike rental:** ¥30 at south gate
- **Sunset:** ~30 minutes before official sunset time for best light
- **Total walk:** 5 km through the city + 1.5 km on the wall = a perfect half-day
