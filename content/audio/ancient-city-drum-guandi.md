# Drum Tower, Bell Tower & Guandi Temple

**Duration:** ~4 minutes
**Location:** Drum Tower area, then Guandi Temple
**Order:** Episode 2 of 10

---

## Script

Walk east from the Four Archways, past the Bell Tower on your left. It's a recent reconstruction, but the original bell — cast in 1451 — is still inside. In Ming Dynasty Datong, the bell marked the opening of the city gates at dawn. The Drum Tower, 200 meters ahead, marked their closing at dusk. Between them, a city's daily rhythm: the bell opens the day, the drum closes it. Every Chinese walled city had this system, but Datong's is one of the best preserved.

The Drum Tower is the taller of the two — three stories, Ming Dynasty brick base with Qing Dynasty wooden upper floors. You can climb it for a view over the old city rooftops. The drum inside is a replica, but the building is authentic.

From the Drum Tower, walk south two blocks to Guandi Temple — the temple of Guan Yu, the god of war. Guan Yu was a real person: a 3rd-century general who became a deity in Chinese folk religion, worshipped as the embodiment of loyalty, righteousness, and martial honor. He's usually depicted with a red face (representing courage), a long black beard, and a guandao — a polearm weapon named after him.

Guandi temples exist in every Chinese city, but Datong's is one of the oldest in Shanxi, with a main hall dating to the Yuan Dynasty (1271–1368). Look up at the ceiling brackets: the woodwork here predates the Ming Dynasty restorations that dominate the rest of the city. This is pre-Ming joinery — rarer, simpler, and in some ways more elegant than what came later.

The temple is small — 15 minutes is enough — but it's an essential piece of Datong's spiritual geography. Guan Yu protects the city. His temple faces the main north-south avenue, positioned so no enemy — human or spirit — could enter without passing his gaze.

## Quick Facts

- **Bell Tower:** Rebuilt, original bell from 1451
- **Drum Tower:** Ming brick base, Qing wooden upper floors, climbable
- **Guandi Temple:** Yuan Dynasty main hall (pre-1368), one of oldest in Shanxi
- **Guan Yu:** 3rd-century general, deified as god of war and loyalty
