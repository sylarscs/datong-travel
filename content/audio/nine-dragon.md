# Nine Dragon Screen: China's Oldest Dragon Wall

## Script

You're looking at the oldest and largest glazed Nine Dragon Screen in China. Built in 1392, early Ming Dynasty, it's 45.5 meters long, 8 meters tall, and 2 meters thick — a giant ceramic mural made of colored glazed tiles, depicting nine dragons playing among clouds and waves.

It was originally the spirit screen for a Ming prince's palace — a feng shui barrier meant to block negative energy from entering the compound. The prince was Zhu Gui, the 13th son of the Hongwu Emperor, the founder of the Ming Dynasty. He was enfeoffed in Datong — sent here to govern the northern frontier — and he built a palace worthy of an emperor's son. The screen is all that remains. The palace burned centuries ago.

The nine dragons are not identical. Each has a different posture, a different expression, a different relationship with the pearl they're chasing — the flaming pearl of wisdom, a Buddhist motif absorbed into Chinese decorative art. The central dragon faces forward, directly at you, while the four on each side face inward. The symmetry is perfect. The craftsmanship — each scale individually molded and glazed — is the work of imperial workshops that no longer exist.

The colors have survived six centuries of northern Chinese winters. The glazes — yellow, blue, green, purple, white — are mineral-based and fired at high temperature, which is why they haven't faded. Look closely at the blue sections: that's cobalt, imported from Persia via the Silk Road. Even in 1392, Datong was connected to the world.

This is a quick stop — 15 minutes is enough — but it's a perfect complement to Huayan Temple, a 5-minute walk away. And it's a preview of the Datong City Wall, which you'll walk next.

## Quick Facts

- **Built:** 1392 AD, early Ming Dynasty
- **Size:** 45.5m long × 8m tall
- **Made of:** Glazed ceramic tiles, cobalt blue from Persia
- **Purpose:** Spirit screen (feng shui barrier) for Prince Zhu Gui's palace
- **Surviving:** The palace is gone; only this screen remains
