# The Catholic Church & Confucian Temple

**Duration:** ~4 minutes
**Location:** Catholic Church (northwest), then Wen Miao (northeast)
**Order:** Episode 4 of 10

---

## Script

From the mosque, walk north through quiet residential streets. You're now in one of the oldest neighborhoods inside the walls — narrow alleys, courtyard homes, laundry drying on balconies. After 10 minutes, you'll reach the Catholic Church of Datong. It's not on most tourist maps. But it's been here since 1889.

The church was built by Belgian missionaries during the late Qing Dynasty, when Datong was opened to foreign traders and evangelists under the unequal treaties. The architecture is Gothic Revival — pointed arches, a rose window, a bell tower — executed in Chinese grey brick. It looks like a French country church that got lost on the way to Amiens and ended up in Shanxi.

The church survived the Boxer Rebellion (which targeted Christian missions), the Japanese occupation, the Cultural Revolution (when it was used as a warehouse), and the recent urban renovation of Datong's old city. It's still an active parish. Sunday Mass is in Chinese, but the doors are open to visitors during the day.

From the church, walk east to Wen Miao — the Confucian Temple. This is the largest Confucian temple in Shanxi, and one of the best preserved. It was built in 1392, the same year as the Nine Dragon Screen, and underwent major restoration in the Qing Dynasty.

Confucian temples are different from Buddhist or Daoist ones. No statues of deities. No incense. No prayers. Instead: steles — stone tablets carved with the names of scholars who passed the imperial examinations. A Confucian temple is a hall of fame for civil servants. The message is clear: scholarship, not faith, is the path to honor.

Wen Miao's main hall has a magnificent double-eaved roof with glazed yellow tiles — imperial yellow, reserved for buildings of the highest status. Inside, a wooden spirit tablet (not a statue) represents Confucius. Flanking him are tablets for his disciples, and the walls are lined with those scholar steles. Walk through slowly. This is where Datong's brightest minds were celebrated for a thousand years.

## Quick Facts

- **Catholic Church:** Built 1889 by Belgian missionaries, Gothic Revival in Chinese brick
- **Wen Miao:** Built 1392, largest Confucian temple in Shanxi
- **Confucian worship:** No statues, no incense — steles with scholars' names
- **Roof:** Imperial yellow glazed tiles (highest status)
