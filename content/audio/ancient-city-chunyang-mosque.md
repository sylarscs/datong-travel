# Chunyang Palace & the Great Mosque

**Duration:** ~4 minutes
**Location:** Chunyang Palace (south), then Great Mosque (west)
**Order:** Episode 3 of 10

---

## Script

From Guandi Temple, walk south to Chunyang Palace — the only Daoist temple inside Datong's ancient city walls. It's small, easily overlooked, and absolutely worth 15 minutes.

Chunyang Palace is dedicated to Lü Dongbin, one of the Eight Immortals of Daoism — a legendary figure who could supposedly travel a thousand miles in the blink of an eye and vanquish demons with a magic sword. The temple was built in the Ming Dynasty, but its current form is Qing — compact, intimate, decorated with murals of the Eight Immortals crossing the sea. The courtyard has a 300-year-old cypress tree. Sit under it for a moment. Daoist temples are designed to make you want to stay.

Now walk west, across the main avenue, into the back streets. In five minutes you'll reach the Great Mosque of Datong — one of the oldest mosques in northern China. This is where the walking tour takes an unexpected turn.

The mosque was founded during the Yuan Dynasty, when Mongol rulers brought Muslim administrators, soldiers, and merchants from Central Asia and the Middle East to govern their Chinese territories. Datong, as a garrison city on the Mongol frontier, had a significant Muslim population. The mosque was their center.

The architecture is a fusion: the prayer hall has a traditional Chinese hip-and-gable roof with flying eaves, but the orientation is toward Mecca, and the decoration is entirely geometric and calligraphic — no human or animal figures, as Islamic tradition requires. Look for the Arabic inscriptions carved into the wooden lintels above the doors, right next to Chinese bracket sets. These two traditions — Chinese woodworking and Islamic geometry — have been coexisting here for 700 years.

The mosque is still active. If it's prayer time, be respectful: don't enter the prayer hall, don't photograph worshippers, and women should cover their heads. Outside of prayer times, visitors are welcome.

## Quick Facts

- **Chunyang Palace:** Daoist temple, Ming/Qing, dedicated to immortal Lü Dongbin
- **Great Mosque:** Founded Yuan Dynasty (~1300s), one of oldest in northern China
- **Style:** Chinese roof + Islamic geometry + Arabic calligraphy
- **Active:** Yes — respect prayer times
