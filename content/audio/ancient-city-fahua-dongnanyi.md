# Fahua Temple & Dongnan Yi

**Duration:** ~4 minutes
**Location:** Fahua Temple and the southeast district (东南邑)
**Order:** Episode 5 of 10

---

## Script

From Wen Miao, walk east into Dongnan Yi — the southeast district. This is the oldest continuously inhabited neighborhood inside the walls. The streets narrow. The buildings lower. The restoration hasn't reached every alley, and you can see the layers: Ming brick foundations, Qing wooden facades, Republican-era shop signs, 1980s tile work, 2020s renovations, all stacked on top of each other like geological strata.

This district was Datong's merchant quarter during the Ming and Qing dynasties. The street names tell the story: Coal Market Street, Grain Store Lane, Silk Alley. For 500 years, traders from across northern China did business here. The courtyards you pass were once counting houses, warehouses, guild halls.

Halfway through the district, you'll reach Fahua Temple — the most underrated temple in Datong. It's small, quiet, and almost never crowded. The temple was built in the Ming Dynasty and restored in the Qing. Its name means "Dharma Flower" — another name for the Lotus Sutra, one of the most influential texts in Mahayana Buddhism.

What makes Fahua special is a single object: a white marble pagoda in the rear courtyard, carved in the Yuan Dynasty style with exquisite relief panels showing scenes from Buddhist scriptures. The pagoda is about 6 meters tall, and every surface is decorated — lotus petals, guardian figures, flying celestials, miniature Buddha niches. It's one of the finest Yuan Dynasty marble carvings in Shanxi, and almost nobody knows it's here.

The temple monks are welcoming. If the gate is open, you can enter. There's no ticket booth, no audio guide, no gift shop. Just a courtyard, a pagoda, and the sound of wind in the old pines.

From Fahua, it's a 5-minute walk north to the Nine Dragon Screen, and another 5 minutes to the south gate of the city wall. You're entering the final stretch of the walking tour.

## Quick Facts

- **Dongnan Yi:** Southeast district, oldest merchant quarter in the city
- **Fahua Temple:** Ming Dynasty, named after the Lotus Sutra
- **White marble pagoda:** Yuan Dynasty style, 6m, exquisite relief carvings
- **Atmosphere:** Quiet, uncrowded, no ticket booth — a hidden gem
