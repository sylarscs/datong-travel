## Script

You're standing at the Four Archways — the exact center of Datong's ancient city. Four decorated gateways, one at each compass point, marking the intersection of the city's two main axes. This has been the heart of Datong for 600 years.

Behind you, to the west, the Bell Tower. Ahead, to the east, the Drum Tower. South, the city wall's Yongtai Gate. North, the road to the old imperial quarter. Everything in the ancient city radiates from this point. If you get lost, find your way back here. The Four Archways have been orienting Datong residents since the Ming Dynasty.

Datong's ancient city is not a museum. It's a living neighborhood where 50,000 people still live, work, pray, and eat. The city wall encloses 3.28 square kilometers — roughly the size of Monaco — and inside that space you'll find Buddhist temples, Daoist shrines, a Confucian temple, a mosque, and a Catholic church, all within walking distance of each other. Name another square mile on Earth with that kind of religious diversity. You can't. That's the point.

This walking tour covers 10 stops, starting here and ending on the city wall at sunset. Total distance: about 5 kilometers. You can do it in an afternoon. The route loops clockwise from the center: east to the Drum Tower and Guandi Temple, south to Chunyang Palace, west to the Mosque, north to the Cathedral and Wen Miao, then east again through the old streets of Dongnan Yi to Fahua Temple, past the Nine Dragon Screen, and up onto the wall for sunset.

Each stop has its own episode. Play them as you walk. Or listen now and walk later — the route works either way.

A practical note before we start: the streets in the ancient city have been renovated. Some people say they're too clean, too new. They have a point. But the layout is authentic. The temples are authentic. And the city that exists between the restored facades — the noodle shops, the morning markets, the grandmothers doing tai chi in the temple courtyards — is completely real. Pay attention to what's between the sights. That's where Datong lives.
