# Datong Ancient City Wall: A 7-Kilometer Walk Through Ming Dynasty History

**Duration:** ~4 minutes
**Location:** City Wall south gate (永泰门, Yongtai Gate)
**Order:** Episode 1 of 3 (Bonus guide — free with 3-guide bundle)

## Script

You're standing at the south gate of Datong's ancient city wall — Yongtai Gate. Behind you, the modern city. In front of you, 7.2 kilometers of Ming Dynasty fortification, fully restored and open to walk, bike, or simply sit on and watch the sunset.

Datong was a military city. For most of its history, it wasn't a tourist destination — it was a garrison. From here, Ming Dynasty soldiers watched the northern horizon for Mongol armies. The wall you're looking at was built in 1372, just four years after the Ming Dynasty was founded. The message was clear: the new dynasty was here, and it was building walls.

The original wall was rammed earth faced with brick. The restoration, completed in the 2010s, is controversial — some say it's too new, too clean, too perfect. And they have a point. The wall you'll walk on is not weathered. It doesn't show its age. But the footprint is authentic, the gates are in their original positions, and the scale is exactly what a Ming Dynasty border city required. Datong was not a backwater. It was one of the most important military cities in the empire.

The wall is 14 meters tall — about the height of a four-story building. The base is 16 meters thick, tapering to 12 meters at the top. It was built to withstand cannon fire (the Ming had gunpowder) and siege towers. The moat outside the wall was originally 10 meters wide and filled with water diverted from the Yuhe River. None of this was decorative. This was defensive architecture at the scale of the Ming Empire's northern border, which was the most militarized frontier on Earth in the 14th century.

You can walk the full 7.2-kilometer loop. It takes about two hours on foot, or 40 minutes by bike. The rental bikes are ¥30 and available at the south gate. If you only have an hour, walk from Yongtai Gate east to the Drum Tower — that's the most scenic stretch, with views of the ancient city on one side and the restored Ming-Qing streets on the other.

The best time to walk is sunset. The wall faces west, and the light hits the brickwork at a low angle, turning it gold. From the top, you can see the Drum Tower, Huayan Temple's pagoda, and the modern skyline beyond. It's one of the best views in Datong, and it's free.

## Quick Facts

- **Built:** 1372 AD, early Ming Dynasty (restored 2010s)
- **Length:** 7.2 kilometers (full loop)
- **Height:** 14m (base 16m thick, top 12m)
- **Purpose:** Military fortification against Mongol incursions
- **Walk time:** ~2 hours (foot) / ~40 min (bike, ¥30 rental)
- **Best time:** Sunset, south gate to Drum Tower stretch
