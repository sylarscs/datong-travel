# Yingxian Wooden Pagoda: The World's Oldest Wooden Skyscraper — Introduction

**Duration:** ~3 minutes
**Location:** 1 hour south of Datong, Yingxian county
**Order:** Episode 1 of 6

---

## Script

You're standing in front of the Sakyamuni Pagoda of Fogong Temple — known to everyone simply as the Yingxian Wooden Pagoda. It's 67 meters tall. It was built in 1056 AD. And it was constructed without a single nail.

This is the oldest and tallest wooden pagoda in the world. Nine stories (five visible from outside, four hidden inside), 54 different types of bracket sets, over 3,000 tons of timber, and a structural system so sophisticated that modern engineers still study it. It has survived 40 major earthquakes, countless storms, and the general wear of nearly a thousand years. It leans slightly — about 2 degrees off vertical — but it still stands.

The pagoda is in Yingxian, a small county town about an hour's drive south of Datong. Most visitors to Datong skip it. That's a mistake. This pagoda is one of the greatest wooden structures ever built, anywhere in the world, in any era. If it were in Japan or Europe, it would be a global icon. Because it's in a small town in Shanxi, it's almost unknown outside China.

Over the next 20 minutes, you'll learn how it was built, why it survives, what's inside, and why the Liao Dynasty — the same Khitan people who built Huayan Temple — created this masterpiece of timber engineering.

This guide covers 6 episodes. You can listen in the car on the drive from Datong — by the time you arrive, you'll know exactly what you're looking at.

## Quick Facts

- **Name:** Sakyamuni Pagoda, Fogong Temple (佛宫寺释迦塔)
- **Built:** 1056 AD, Liao Dynasty
- **Height:** 67.31 meters (≈22 stories)
- **Material:** Timber, no nails — entirely mortise-and-tenon joinery
- **Status:** Oldest and tallest wooden pagoda in the world
- **Earthquakes survived:** 40+ major events
