## Beijing to Datong: Easier Than You Think

The high-speed train from Beijing to Datong takes just **1 hour and 45 minutes** — faster than getting from central Beijing to the Great Wall at Badaling. You can leave Beijing after breakfast and be standing in front of 1,500-year-old Buddhist cave carvings by lunch.

This guide covers everything: train types, stations, how to book, and practical tips for a smooth journey.

## Which Station in Beijing?

This is the most common mistake. There are **two stations** with Beijing-Datong trains:

| Station | Train Type | Travel Time | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Beijing North (北京北站, Běijīng Běi Zhàn)** | High-speed (G-train) | 1h 45min | ~30/day |
| **Beijing Fengtai (北京丰台站)** | High-speed (G-train) | 2h | ~10/day |

**Beijing North (北京北站)** is your best bet. It's on Metro Line 2/4/13 (Xizhimen station, 西直门), very central. More trains, slightly faster.

**Beijing Fengtai (北京丰台站)** is newer and less crowded, but further south. Only use this if you're staying in southern Beijing or Beijing North trains are sold out.

<InfoBox type="warning" title="Important">
Do NOT go to Beijing South (北京南站) or Beijing West (北京西站). They don't have Datong trains. Taxi drivers may not be familiar with Datong as a destination, so have this written down: **我要去大同 (Wǒ yào qù Dàtóng)**.
</InfoBox>

## Train Types & Prices

| Class | Price | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Second Class (二等座)** | ¥170–250 | Comfortable, 2+3 seating. Plenty good enough for 2 hours. |
| **First Class (一等座)** | ¥280–370 | Wider seats, 2+2 layout, more legroom. Nice but unnecessary for this short trip. |
| **Business Class (商务座)** | ¥530–650 | Lie-flat seats, lounge access. Overkill for under 2 hours. |

**Recommendation:** Second class is perfectly comfortable for 1h 45min. Save the ¥100+ for noodles in Datong.

## How to Book Tickets

### Option 1: Trip.com (Easiest for Foreigners)

1. Go to Trip.com → Trains
2. Search "Beijing" to "Datong"
3. Select your train and class
4. Pay with Visa/Mastercard
5. Receive e-ticket confirmation — show your passport at the station

**No need to print anything.** China railways use passport-based e-ticketing. Just tap your passport at the gate. This works at both Beijing North and Datong South.

### Option 2: 12306 Official App

The official China Railway app now has an English version. Cheaper (no service fee), but requires a Chinese phone number for registration. If you have one, this saves ¥10–20 per ticket.

### Option 3: Buy at the Station

You can buy tickets at the station on the day, but **this is risky** — popular departures sell out, especially on Friday evenings and weekends. Book at least 1–2 days ahead.

## Step-by-Step: At Beijing North Station

1. **Arrive by Metro:** Take Line 2, 4, or 13 to Xizhimen (西直门). Exit A or D. The station entrance is directly connected to the metro.
2. **Security check:** All Chinese train stations have airport-style security. Bags go through X-ray. Takes 5–15 minutes depending on queues.
3. **Find your gate:** Check the departure board for your train number (e.g., G2501). Gates are numbered and clearly marked in English.
4. **Boarding:** Gates open ~15 minutes before departure. Tap your passport on the e-gate scanner. It reads the chip — hold it flat against the scanner.
5. **Find your seat:** Carriage and seat number are on your e-ticket. Luggage goes above your seat or at the end of the carriage.

<InfoBox type="tip" title="Pro Tip">
Arrive at the station **30–45 minutes before departure**. Security lines at Chinese stations can be unpredictable. If you miss your train, tickets are usually non-refundable, but you can exchange for a later train at the ticket counter (small fee, subject to availability).
</InfoBox>

## Arriving in Datong

You'll arrive at **Datong South Railway Station (大同南站)**, about 8km from the ancient city.

| Transport | Time | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| **Taxi** | 20 min | ¥25–35 |
| **Didi** (via Alipay) | 20 min | ¥20–30 |
| **Bus 14** | 40 min | ¥2 |
| **Bus 66** | 35 min | ¥2 |

**Recommendation:** Take a taxi or Didi. After a train ride, you don't want to figure out the bus system. Taxis queue outside the arrivals hall — just follow the signs.

Show the driver your hotel address in Chinese. If staying in the ancient city, say: **"Qù gǔ chéng"** (去古城), or show them: **请带我去古城鼓楼附近 (Please take me to the Drum Tower area of the ancient city).**

## Day Trip from Beijing: Can You Do It?

**Yes, but it's a long day.** Here's the math:

- 6:30 AM: Leave hotel in Beijing
- 7:30 AM: Train from Beijing North
- 9:15 AM: Arrive Datong South, taxi to Yungang Grottoes
- 9:45 AM – 12:30 PM: Yungang Grottoes
- 12:30 PM: Lunch
- 1:30 PM: Huayan Temple + Nine Dragon Screen
- 3:30 PM: City Wall quick walk
- 4:30 PM: Head to station
- 5:30 PM: Train back to Beijing
- 7:15 PM: Arrive Beijing

You'll see the grottoes and the ancient city, but miss the Hanging Temple (too far for a day trip). It's a 12-hour day door-to-door. Doable, but an overnight stay is much more enjoyable.

## Common Questions

### Can I use my passport as ID for train tickets?

Yes. Your passport IS your train ticket. When booking, enter your name exactly as it appears on your passport. At the station, tap your passport on the e-gate scanner — the chip in your passport cover is read automatically. No paper ticket needed.

### What if I miss my train?

Go to the ticket counter (售票处) immediately. You can exchange for a later train on the same route, subject to availability. There's usually a small fee (¥10–30). Tickets are not fully refundable once the train has departed.

### Is there WiFi on the train?

Yes, all high-speed trains have free WiFi. Connect to "CRH-WiFi" and enter your passport number. It's decent for messaging and browsing, but not fast enough for video streaming. Download offline content before boarding.

### Can I bring luggage on the train?

Yes. There's no strict luggage limit on Chinese high-speed trains. Overhead racks fit standard carry-on bags, and there are larger luggage racks at the end of each carriage. Large suitcases are fine.

### Are there toilets on the train?

Yes, Western and squat toilets at the end of each carriage. Bring your own tissues and hand sanitizer — they're not always stocked.
