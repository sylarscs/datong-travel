## Why Visit Datong?

Datong (大同) is one of northern China's best-kept secrets. While tourists crowd into Beijing's Forbidden City and Xi'an's Terracotta Warriors, this Shanxi province city quietly holds three of China's most remarkable sights — and you can see them all in a weekend.

Here's what makes Datong special:

- **Yungang Grottoes** — 51,000+ Buddhist statues carved into a 1km sandstone cliff. UNESCO World Heritage. One of China's "Three Great Cave Art Sites" alongside Mogao and Longmen.
- **Hanging Temple (Xuankong Si)** — A 1,500-year-old monastery clinging to a vertical cliff face, 75 meters in the air. There is literally nothing else like it.
- **Datong Ancient City** — Fully restored Ming Dynasty walls (7.2km loop), Liao Dynasty temples, and the oldest Nine Dragon Screen in China.
- **The Food** — Shanxi is the birthplace of knife-cut noodles (刀削面), and Datong does them better than anywhere. This alone is worth the train ride.

Datong is also an ideal stop on the **Beijing → Datong → Pingyao → Xi'an** route — one of the most rewarding 2-week itineraries in China.

<InfoBox type="highlight" title="Best Time to Visit">
April–June and September–October are ideal: mild weather (15–25°C), clear skies, and fewer domestic tourists. Avoid the first week of October (National Day Golden Week) — everywhere will be packed.
</InfoBox>

## What to Eat in Datong

Datong is in Shanxi province, famous for its wheat-based cuisine. Here's what you must try:

1. **Knife-Cut Noodles (刀削面)** — The signature dish. Noodles are shaved directly from a block of dough into boiling water. Served with braised pork, pickled vegetables, and chili oil. Try them at **Dongfang Xiaomian (东方削面)**, a local chain that's been serving perfect bowls for 30+ years.

2. **Datong Lamb Soup (大同羊杂)** — Rich, peppery lamb offal soup. A winter breakfast staple. Find it at any morning market or small eatery with a queue of locals.

3. **Youmian Kaolaolao (莜面栲栳栳)** — Steamed oat noodles rolled into honeycomb shapes, dipped in garlic-vinegar sauce. A Shanxi specialty.

4. **Liangfen (凉粉)** — Cold mung bean jelly strips with sesame paste, vinegar, and chili. Refreshing on hot summer days. Hunyuan county (near the Hanging Temple) is famous for this.

5. **Shaomai (烧麦)** — Datong-style shaomai are larger than the Cantonese version, filled with pork and scallions, pan-fried until crispy on the bottom.

<InfoBox type="tip">
Don't expect English menus. Take photos of dishes you want to try (we recommend searching 大同美食 on Xiaohongshu before your trip), or point at what other tables are eating. Locals are friendly and will help you order.
</InfoBox>

## Getting Around Datong

The city is spread out. Key distances:

- Datong South Railway Station → Ancient City: ~8km (taxi ¥25–35, 20 min)
- Ancient City → Yungang Grottoes: ~16km (Bus 3, ¥2, 50 min; taxi ¥40–50, 30 min)
- Ancient City → Hanging Temple: ~65km (bus from Datong Bus Station ¥30, 1.5h; private car ¥400–600 round trip including waiting time)

For most trips within the city, **Didi** (Chinese Uber, available inside the Alipay app) is the easiest option.

## Suggested 3-Day Datong Itinerary

**Day 1: The Big One**
- Morning: Yungang Grottoes (arrive at opening, 8:30 AM, to beat the crowds)
- Lunch: Dongfang Xiaomian for knife-cut noodles
- Afternoon: Huayan Temple (1.5h) + Nine Dragon Screen (30 min)
- Evening: Walk the City Wall at sunset, dinner in the ancient city

**Day 2: The Cliff Temple**
- Morning: Drive to Hanging Temple (leave at 7:30 AM, arrive by 9 AM)
- Climb the temple walkways (1–1.5h)
- Lunch: Liangfen in Hunyuan village
- Afternoon: Return to Datong, visit Shanhua Temple (善化寺, free, less crowded than Huayan)
- Evening: Night market near the Drum Tower

**Day 3: Deeper Cuts**
- Morning: Yingxian Wooden Pagoda (应县木塔), 1h drive — the oldest wooden pagoda in the world, built in 1056 without a single nail
- Afternoon: Datong Museum (free, excellent English signage) or Coal Museum (quirky but fascinating)
- Evening: Hot pot at a local spot, final walk on the city wall

## Practical Quick Tips

<InfoBox type="warning" title="Before You Go">
1. **Set up Alipay** with your international card before arriving. China is nearly cashless.
2. **Install a VPN** before entering China. Google, Instagram, WhatsApp are all blocked.
3. **Download offline maps**. Google Maps has poor China coverage. Apple Maps works. For Android, screenshot routes in advance.
4. **Book train tickets in advance** on Trip.com (English interface, accepts international cards). High-speed trains sell out on weekends and holidays.
5. **Carry your passport**. You'll need it for train tickets, hotel check-in, and attraction entry.
</InfoBox>
